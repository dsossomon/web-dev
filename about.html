About Me

My name is Andrew Branson Garner Sossomon, but I prefer to go by Drew. I am 16 years old, and I have cerebral palsy and left-side hemiplegia. I was born at 24 weeks as a micro preemie, weighing just 1 pound 11 ounces. From the very beginning I’ve been defying the odds. I started to play soccer when I was a young kid, around three years old. I have played soccer for 13 years now, and the last 8 years I've been a self-taught goalkeeper. Ever since then, I’ve pursued the sport with dedication and perseverance. My condition has affected the mobility and strength on my left side, making simple drills and workouts challenging, having to exert twice more energy to catch up to my teammates on a run or in general, yet soccer has always been the field where my resilience and hard work overshadow my physical limitations. It's more than a game to me; it's where I've cultivated resilience, discipline, dedication, and the power to never give up.

I am currently a sophomore at Youngsville Academy, where I balance my commitment to soccer with my academic responsibilities. My strongest subjects are history and science, and I particularly enjoy math, especially geometry even though I haven't done well, which I’m taking this year. Literature is also a subject where I typically perform well. Throughout my academic journey, I’ve worked hard to become as independent as possible. When I started high school, I stopped needing to be pulled out of class for additional support, which was a major milestone for me. My IEP provides me with extended time on tests, which helps me demonstrate my knowledge effectively. I’m proud of my growth in becoming a more independent learner while managing my cerebral palsy. My first two years of high school were academically challenging. Despite studying hard, I struggled with taking tests and received poor grades on my exams.

However, this semester, I’ve focused on improving my grades and developing better study habits. Just as I’ve learned on the soccer field, I understand that setbacks are opportunities to work harder and come back stronger. I approach my academics with the same determination and perseverance that I bring to goalkeeping. I’m currently exploring several fields of study for college, including agriculture, business management, and computer science. I’m keeping my options open as I continue to discover my interests and strengths. I’m committed to being a true student-athlete who excels both in the classroom and on the field, and I look forward to finding a college program that supports both my academic and athletic goals.

When I was little, I had to wear a brace to stretch out the muscles in my left leg because they were getting super tight. In elementary school, I would get bullied for being different from everybody else. I would get picked on for getting questions wrong, and I was pushed down the stairs. I would get pulled out of class to work on my speech, handwriting, and for physical therapy. During my last two years of middle school at Wake Preparatory Academy, I was cut from my school team because I had a disability and because I didn't play for my school coach's club team.

I refused to give up. I remember struggling for weeks to master dribbling through the cones with the left side of my body, catching the ball mid-air, and working on my diving. Every day during recess at school, I would practice dribbling the ball through the cones and passing the ball against the wall, and I would practice my diving on the grass repeatedly while everybody else was playing with each other or talking with each other. Even at home, I would continue to either practice dribbling in my room through the cones without a ball, or I would toss the ball up in the air while lying on my back and catching the ball if it was raining outside. If it wasn't raining, I would go out after my homework and practice diving, punting the ball, and work on my kicking. In my freshman year of high school, I made the first-ever varsity men's soccer team for my school, Youngsville Academy, and I was the only player with a disability to make the team.

As a goalkeeper, my condition has given me a unique perspective. It has forced me to work harder on my left-side footwork and understand that communication and hard work are just as vital as making a great save. I’ve made a significant impact on my past and recent club teams not just with saves, but with leadership. I started to play as a goalkeeper in 2017, playing for SWAT, which was the last position that I had ever played before, and I wanted to give it a try. In my first season as a goalkeeper, I made 9 assists, 200 saves with a goals-against average of 0.63 and a save percentage of 95.2% across 16 games with a total of 15 shutouts. Within those 16 games, when it came down to penalty shootouts, I blocked every shot that I faced, and I didn't miss a single one. After that season, I wanted to continue playing as a goalkeeper.

In 2022, I made 120 saves with a goals-against average of 1.25 and a save percentage of 92.3% playing for NBT soccer academy. My most recent season with Wake FC 09 Fulham presented a tougher challenge and stronger opposition, resulting in a higher goals-against average of 4.68. However, my resilience held strong, demonstrated by 348 saves and a save percentage of 81.5% across 17 games in regulation and 9 games in USL pre-season and end of season tournaments. I’ve continued this momentum with the Wake FC 2009 NPL North Academy team, achieving a perfect goals-against average of 0.00 and a save percentage of 100.0% in 1 Game so far.

Recently, on September 27th, 2025, during a scrimmage for my club team, Wake FC’s 2009 NPL North Academy team, I suffered a torn ACL, Meniscus and ILL tear in my right knee. This injury has sidelined me, but I have already begun my bounce - back journey. I am starting to exercise and work harder so that when I'm cleared in October or November of 2026, I can return to my position in goal stronger than before.

My journey has taught me that the only true limitations are the ones we accept, and I refuse to accept any. I am looking for a college program that values not just raw athletic talent, but the character built through adversity. I believe my resilience, work ethic, and unique perspective as a goalkeeper make me a valuable asset. I want to be part of a team where I can make a huge impact not only with my saves but with my attitude and leadership, pushing my teammates and myself to achieve our best every day. My goal is to be a leader on and off the field, using my unique experience to demonstrate that commitment and communication can overcome any obstacle. I am ready to bring my dedication, my discipline, and my love for the game to your program and to continue defying expectations and soon play professional soccer and play for the USMNT once graduating from college.
